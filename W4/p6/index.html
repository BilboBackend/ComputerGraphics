a) What is the difference between Phong shading and Phong lighting (the Phong reflection model)?

The Phong ligthing is the model for how the light is reflected given a surface, a light source and a viewer, whereas the shading is the application of lighting model to every pixel in the render.

b) What is the difference between flat shading, Gouraud shading, and Phong shading? List pros and cons of each. Is Gouraud or Phong shading the best method for simulating highlights? Explain.

Flat shading is the simplest, where each triangle gets it's own color. In the Gouraud shading each vertex has a normal and we calculate the color of a triangle by interpolating over these normals. In the Phong we do as in Gouraud but instead of interpolating over triangles we do it over the pixels.
This means that the shaders are increasingly smooth looking in the order: Flat, Gouraud, Phong. The downside is that the shaders are computationally more requiring in the same order, so Phong is most inefficient etc.

c) What is the difference between a directional light and a point light?

A point light is located at a specific position and emits light all around it sort of like a lightbulb. A directional light on the other hand is located infinetely far away but has one specific direction in which it shines, sort of like a flashlight.

d) Does the eye position influence the shading of an object in any way?

Yes, since the angle between the light source and the viewer changes this will result in movement of the specular highlighting.

e) What is the effect of setting the specular term to (0, 0, 0)?

This basically nullfies the specular term and as such the Phong and Gouraud shading becomes equivalent.
